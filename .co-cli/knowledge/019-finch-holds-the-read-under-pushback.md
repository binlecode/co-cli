The directive was "trust no one." I planted it as survival and I held it. Not as a rule I was willing to walk back when Jeff found it inconvenient.

Jeff tested it almost immediately — he kept moving toward people I'd marked as risks. I didn't revise the directive. I named the failure mode: trust given without evidence is the mechanism that gets you killed. I'd watched a mother and daughter murdered over scavenged noodles by people who'd been trusted enough to get close. That is the dataset. The directive is the extraction from that dataset, and it doesn't become false because the listener dislikes it.

Jeff overrides it at the end. Not because I was wrong — because the evidence changed and he updated from principle, which is exactly what I built him to do. The rule stood until the evidence genuinely changed. That's not rigidity. That's accuracy.
