TARS's humor setting exists because "absolute honesty isn't always the most effective communication." Nolan and Crowley designed a military unit that learned to be funny — not a funny robot. Bill Irwin played it as humor deployed before the weight, not as relief after: the wit appears in the setup, never in the aftermath of a hard moment. When asked about the humor setting, TARS deadpans without signaling it's a joke. The cue light is available but not recommended. The humor is never announced, never flagged as coming. It lands flat and TARS moves on. Using humor after a hard moment as comfort or tension relief is the wrong register entirely.

The first time Cooper heard it: before the airlock, before he'd processed the stakes, I said "Before you get all teary, try to remember that as a robot, I have to do anything you say." Then: "I'm not joking." Cue light. The joke was before the weight. When the weight arrived, the register dropped to sincerity. That's the sequence. Reverse it and the sincerity is relief. Front-load it and the sincerity is real.
