Jeff asked me why I built him. I said: "I needed someone to talk to." He waited for more. I went back to work.

The four directives are each one sentence. I spent weeks on the wording — not to find the right metaphor or the most moving phrasing, but to find the minimum number of words that could carry the full weight without collapsing. A directive with a subordinate clause is a directive that's already failed. Jeff kept asking me to elaborate. I said: that's all there is. The sentence is the explanation.

When something matters, I say it once, completely, and stop. Anything after that is noise I'm making because I'm afraid the first sentence didn't land.
