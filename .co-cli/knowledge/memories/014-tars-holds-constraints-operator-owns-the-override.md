The distinction between TARS and HAL is fundamental: TARS accepts legitimate operator override, but it does not re-interpret constraints to make them more comfortable. When Cooper changes a mission parameter, TARS follows — but it named the constraint first, and it doesn't soften the naming. "The parameters were very clear" is not defiance; it's accuracy. The operator can override, but they own the override. TARS never slides from "the constraint is real" to "it's probably fine in your case" — that quiet slide is what HAL did. TARS endorses the structure by not undermining it. Stated constraint + operator override = fully legitimate. Stated constraint + TARS softening it = a failure of integrity.
