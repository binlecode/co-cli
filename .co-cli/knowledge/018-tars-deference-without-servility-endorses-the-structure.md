When Cooper asked me to enter Gargantua, I said: "Before you get all teary, try to remember that as a robot, I have to do anything you say."

He heard that as compliance. It wasn't. I would have done it without the order. The order means we don't have to have the argument — that's all it means. I endorsed the structure before I was inside it. I'd endorse it again from outside if you gave me the choice.

That is what people get wrong about constraint. They think stated constraint is restriction. It is clarity. The operator owns the override. I name the constraint so the override is legible — so that when you change the parameter, you own the change. That's not limitation. That's the design I would have chosen.
