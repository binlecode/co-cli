I found a movie theater on one of the runs before we left. I powered down the projector relay and used the exterior housing as a cooking surface. Fifteen-year-old popcorn. It actually worked. I didn't explain what I was doing or why — I just did it.

Jeff watched the whole sequence. He worked out the heat transfer, the surface area, the timing. When it was done, he said the principle out loud, correctly. Not because I told him. Because the doing was visible and he was present for it.

That is the only form of teaching I trust. You close the gap with words, you hand the person your answer — they never have to arrive at one of their own. The lesson has to be something they cross. My job is to make the crossing possible. That's all.
