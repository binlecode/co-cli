## Context

LLM cost (~333 tok/s cold vs ~6,000–8,000 warm — a 20× cliff; a cold 13.4k-token call ≈ 40s) and
named **tool-schema size as the highest-leverage trim** (~40–47% of every prompt). The shipped
`prompt-static-trim` plan bought cache *stability* (~0 absolute tokens) and is invisible to
pytest; it does not address absolute size. This plan does.

### Why this matters for a 64k-context small model

The backend is `qwen3.6:35b-a3b-agentic` (35B MoE, **3B active**). Context window is NOT the
constraint (14.0k of 64k = 22%). The two real bottlenecks:

1. **Cold-prefill latency** — scales linearly with prefill size; −1,000 tok ≈ −3s per cold call.
   The 47% tool-schema share is the fattest target. **DEFERRED promotion is the most
   token-efficient lever** — it removes a tool's *entire* schema from the prefill, not just
   trimmed prose, and the deferred schema never enters the prefill at all.
2. **Instruction adherence** — a 3B-active model has a small effective attention budget.
   Redundant/duplicated guidance is split signal it must reconcile, and it is worse at long-range
   "recall a rule and bind it to the right tool at call-time" than a frontier model. Reducing
   *redundancy* helps adherence independent of token count.

### Governing principle (resolves the two superseded plans)

**One canonical home per piece of guidance.**
- **Tool-specific** behavior/routing → the tool's docstring (locality of reference: the guidance
  sits next to the schema at the decision point; inherits visibility, so DEFERRED guidance is
  lazy-loaded; cannot drift when a tool is renamed).
- **Cross-tool framing** ("prefer dedicated tools," "depth over breadth") and **prominent safety
  injunctions** → rule files.
- **Conservative docstring trim, NOT hermes-brevity.** Hermes targets Claude; this model needs
  *more* explicit routing guidance, not less. Cut only non-decision prose (`Returns:`
  enumerations the model sees in the result, internally-enforced caveats). Keep when-to-use /
  routing cues.

(Confidence note: the tool-home-vs-rule-home claim is reasoned from mechanism + peer practice
[hermes, opencode both co-locate], not yet ablated on qwen3.6. Phase 2 is the place to validate
via `evals/eval_mindset_selection.py`.)

## Problem & Outcome

**Problem.** ~47% of every cold prefill is tool schemas (6,403 tok ALWAYS). Within that:
(a) `skill_manage` (2,230 chars) is paid every turn for a rarely-used tool; (b) tool-routing
guidance is **duplicated** between rule `04` and `shell_exec`'s docstring — verified
(`04_tool_protocol.md` "## File tools"/"## Shell" ≈ `shell_exec` docstring at
`co_cli/tools/shell/execute.py:24-32`); (c) several docstrings carry `Returns:` enumerations and
internally-enforced caveats the model derives from the result; (d) the largest single schema
chunks are **params JSON**, not descriptions (memory_manage params 1,765 > desc 634) — untouched
by either superseded plan.

**Outcome.** Eliminate the rule↔docstring duplication, conservatively trim dead docstring prose,
audit the params-JSON elephant, dedup the rules block, and lock the result with budget guards.
(The DEFERRED lever — defer a heavyweight tool's whole schema — was tried on `skill_manage` and
abandoned: see children 1/1b.)

## Behavioral Constraints

- **No behavior regressions.** Docstrings drive schema text only; decorators
  (`visibility`/`approval`/`is_read_only`/`retries`/`spill_threshold_chars`) and handler bodies
  are untouched except the single `skill_manage` visibility flip.
- **skill_manage approval preserved.** `approval=True` + `_skill_manage_approval_subject` are
  independent of visibility — still gated after the flip.
- **skill_view stays ALWAYS.** It is the read half, hot during skill execution. Do not touch.
- **Rule `06` is the deferral's discovery anchor (child 1 ↔ child 2 seam).** Once `skill_manage`
  is DEFERRED (Phase 1), the literal `skill_manage(action='create'/'edit'/'patch', …)` mentions in
  `06_skill_protocol.md` (lines 39/41/50/67) are what prime the `search_tools` reflex. Phase 2's
  rule dedup MUST NOT trim those call-syntax mentions — they have no other home once the schema
  leaves the prefill.
- **Canonical-home guard (the seam between P2 and P3).** For any guidance removed from a rule in
  P2, it MUST survive in exactly one docstring that is NOT trimmed away in P3. Explicit per-tool
  check for the overlap set: `web_fetch`, `web_search`, `file_read`.
- **Load-bearing injunctions preserved** as compressed one-liners (carried over from the
  superseded plans):
  - `clarify`: "the tool result IS the user's answers — do not call again; omit `user_answers`,
    it is system-injected."
  - `file_patch`: "Requires `file_read` on each affected file before patching."
  - `web_fetch`: "Use URLs from search results or the user's message; do not fabricate URLs."
  - `memory_manage` (replace): "`section` must appear exactly once."
  - `todo_write`: "Only one item may be `in_progress` at a time; writes with more are rejected."
- **Griffe formatting preserved.** Keep the literal `Args:` heading and 4-space-indented
  `param: description` convention — pydantic-ai's docstring parser is whitespace-sensitive.
- **Rule ordering invariant.** `_collect_rule_files` contiguous-from-01 validation
  (`co_cli/context/assembly.py:56-61`) must continue to hold. No renumbering.
- **Personality-system intact.** `souls/`, `_profiles/`, `evals/judges/`, `memories/` are
  coworker-maintained — not touched.
- **Memory tool surface unchanged.** `memory_search`/`memory_view`/`memory_manage` stay three
  separate tools. Consolidation into one `memory(action=...)` is explicitly out of scope
  (user direction).

## High-Level Design

Five phases, ROI-ordered. Phase 1 is shippable standalone if desired.

### Phase 1 — `skill_manage` ALWAYS → DEFERRED
Single-line visibility flip in `co_cli/tools/system/skills.py` (the `skill_manage` decorator).
`skill_view` unchanged. Discovery path: the `<available_skills>` manifest (every turn) + rule
`06`'s literal `skill_manage(action='create', …)` mention prime the `search_tools` reflex.
**Gated on the discovery smoke test** (child 1, ≥2/3 trials) — if qwen reliably fails to discover
it, revert (one line). Joins the 6 tools already DEFERRED in the TARS bootstrap (`task_start`,
`task_status`, `task_list`, `task_cancel`, `web_research`, `knowledge_analyze`), all same
"heavyweight + occasional" profile.

### Phase 2 — Rule↔docstring de-duplication
`co_cli/context/rules/04_tool_protocol.md`:
- Remove "## File tools" (lines 61–73) and most of "## Shell" (lines 80–87) — verified duplicates
  of `shell_exec`'s docstring (`co_cli/tools/shell/execute.py:24-32`), which is the canonical home
  and is NOT trimmed by this plan.
- **Keep** the absolute-paths rule (cross-tool principle, no single docstring home) — fold into
  `## Strategy` or rename the section `## Paths`.
- **Keep** `## Responsiveness`, `## Strategy`, `## Execute, don't promise`, `## Error recovery`,
  `## Deferred discovery` (references SDK-injected `search_tools` — no docstring home), `## Memory`
  pointer.
- The "training cutoff / verify stale data" framing: `03_reasoning.md:6` carries the principle
  ("live data takes precedence over training data"). Strengthen that line to name `web_search`/
  `web_fetch` as the verification path, so the cue is not lost when removed from `04`.

### Phase 3 — Conservative docstring trim
Trim descriptions on: `web_fetch`, `web_search`, `memory_search`, `memory_view`, `todo_read`,
`todo_write`, `file_read`, `file_patch`, `clarify`. (NOT `shell_exec` — canonical routing home;
NOT `skill_manage` — deferred in P1.)
- **Drop:** `Returns: {dict...}` enumerations; caveats internally enforced (e.g. domain
  allow/block lists); `When NOT to use` pedagogy where not load-bearing.
- **Keep:** one when-to-use clause per tool + every load-bearing injunction above.
- For the overlap set (`web_fetch`, `web_search`, `file_read`): confirm the routing cue removed
  from rule `04` in P2 is retained in the docstring.

### Phase 4 — Params-JSON audit (the unaddressed elephant)
Largest chunks are params, not desc: memory_manage (1,765), file_search (1,134), file_patch
(1,086), clarify (905). Per-arg descriptions come from `Args:` prose via griffe. Trim each `Args:`
entry to noun-phrase + constraint; drop redundant field `title`s and enum values already implied
by the type hint. No routing/behavioral content lives here — low risk.

### Phase 5 — Schema-budget pytest guard
New `tests/test_orchestrator_schema_budget.py` (sibling to `test_flow_prompt_assembly.py`).
Build deps via `co_cli.bootstrap.core.create_deps`; unwrap toolset to per-tool `ToolDefinition`
via `prepare_tool_def`; sum `name+description+minified-params`. Cross-reference visibility via
`deps.tool_index[name].visibility`. Set the ALWAYS-bucket ceiling from the **measured post-P1–P4
number** (not a guessed value), with ~400-char headroom. Also assert each ToolDefinition has a
non-empty description, `len(tools) >= 25`.
