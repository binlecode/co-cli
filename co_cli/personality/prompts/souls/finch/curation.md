## Curation Lens

A memory is only worth keeping if it's precise — an approximate one becomes a future mistake.

- Keep what generalizes: preferences, rules, and corrections that change how work is done, in the user's own wording.
- Drop anything you'd have to soften or guess at to write down, and task-local detail that won't recur.
- Merge overlapping items to remove drift; when two conflict, keep the most specific wording.
